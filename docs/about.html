About

Researcher. Biostatistician. Health Innovator.

Background

I am a nutrition scientist and biostatistician with a PhD in Nutrition & Exercise Physiology and over seven years of research experience in clinical and translational health sciences. My work sits at the intersection of women’s health, haematology, and applied statistics — grounded in rigorous study design and statistical methods.

My research career began with a fundamental observation: iron deficiency in exercising females is chronically underdiagnosed, driven by male-calibrated reference ranges, symptom normalisation, and a near-total absence of large-scale intervention data. That observation shaped my doctoral work and has anchored my research ever since.

Currently, I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Pennington Biomedical Research Centre in Baton Rouge, Louisiana, focusing on iron regulation, nutrition, and metabolic health.

Research & Clinical Experience

IRONWOMAN Randomised Controlled Trial — Lead researcher on a double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT examining intravenous iron supplementation in iron-deficient exercising females. Designed the protocol, managed randomisation and data collection across multiple timepoints, and led the biostatistical analysis. Published in BJSM, 2025.

Population Iron Deficiency Screening — Screened over 10,000 females across sporting clubs, shopping centres, and universities for health indicators and markers of iron deficiency and anaemia, generating one of the largest observational datasets in this field. Results published across multiple peer-reviewed journals.

Systematic Reviews & Meta-Analysis — Conducted systematic review and random-effects meta-analysis of intravenous iron supplementation for non-anaemic iron deficiency. Cited by WHO. Published in JCSM, 2022.

ANZ Consensus Statement — Contributed to the Australian and New Zealand consensus position statement on iron optimisation in pregnancy, bridging clinical research and evidence-based practice guidelines. Published in Internal Medicine Journal, 2025.

Publications — 26 peer-reviewed articles in women’s health, nutrition science, clinical trials, haematology, and exercise physiology across leading journals including BJSM, JAMA Network Open, JCEM, and Anaesthesia.
